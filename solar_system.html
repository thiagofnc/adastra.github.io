Sun

The principal body and the most voluminous in the entire Solar System, it is equivalent to 1.3 million planet Earths! Fun facts: its orbital velocity is not uniform throughout its structure, and it creates a series of effects in our atmosphere, one of which is the Polar Auroras, through the production of solar winds.

Mercury

It is one of the smallest planets in the system and the closest to the Sun, having approximately 1/3 the diameter of the Earth (equivalent to about 5,000 km).Curiosities: it has the fastest orbital rotation and, due to its proximity to the Sun, its surface is extremely hot, reaching 430 degrees a day.

Venus

Even though it is not the closest to the Sun,it is the hottest planet in the system,its atmosphere is made up mostly of greenhouse gases.Curiosities: it has no moons, its rotation occurs in the opposite way to all planets in the system and its volume is 1.1 times smaller than that of Earth.

Earth

It is the 3rd planet in the solar system and it is the only one known to have life. It has the shape of a geoid and a single moon. Curiosities: 70% of its surface is covered by water, and the moon influences the formation of the tides on the planet, due to its gravitational force.

Mars

It's about half the size of Earth. It has a reddish appearance due to the composition of its rocks (composed of iron oxides). Curiosities:it has two moons and an average temperature of -60Â°C, in addition to the fact that there are indications of the past existence of liquid water,based on some features observed on its surface.

Jupiter

It is the largest planet in the solar system, so large that its volume is 1321 times greater than that of Earth. Curiosities: it has the most moons in its orbit, 92 in total and a red spot, which is actually a 650 km/h wind storm.

Saturn

It is the second largest planet in the solar system and its volume is approximately 763 times greater than that of Earth. Its main characteristic is thousands of rings. Curiosities: it has 83 moons in its orbit, is composed of gas and moves slowly compared to others in the solar system.

Uranus

It is yet another gaseous planet in the solar system, with a volume about 63 times larger than Earth. It also has a large number of moons in its orbit, 27 in total. Curiosity: they have unusual axial rotation for turning practically sideways.

Neptune

The last planet in the solar system, marked by its dark blue color reminiscent of the ocean and for being the smallest gaseous planet in the solar system. Its volume is about 57 times larger than Earth's. Curiosity: it has 14 moons in its orbit.
